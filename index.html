About Me

What I Work On

I am interested in the overlap between first-principles thinking, software systems, and scientific research. A lot of my work starts with a question that seems slightly too annoying or too niche to leave alone, then turns into a tool, a paper, or a longer thread of investigation.

That has recently taken the form of multi-account CLI tooling, productivity software, materials-property prediction research, and ongoing notes around AI, compression, and neuroscience. I care less about sounding finished and more about building work that is careful, testable, and worth revisiting.

Current Focus

Building practical developer tooling with strong systems ergonomics.

Researching small-data, parameter-efficient deep learning architectures.

Publishing work and maintaining a public trail of notes, experiments, and source code.

Munchkin

A cross-platform multi-account manager for Codex, Gemini, and Qwen CLIs. It stores sessions in an encrypted local vault, supports fast switching, checks session health, tracks Codex quota state, and can route commands through quota-aware load balancing.
