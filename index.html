Who I Am

About Me

Me

I'm driven by curiosity and a habit of digging into problems a little deeper than necessary. Most of my interests sit at the intersection of science, technology, and creative work, where I enjoy understanding how things work before trying to build on top of them. I tend to approach problems from first principles, spending time asking why something behaves the way it does before moving on to how it can be implemented or improved.

Lately, that curiosity has taken the form of exploring ideas in information theory and compression, building small productivity and systems tools, and experimenting with music as another way of thinking and expressing ideas. This space is a collection of projects, notes, and experiments that reflect what I'm learning over time rather than finished answers. I don't have everything figured out, but I enjoy the process of working carefully, questioning assumptions, and improving incrementally.

Currently

Exploring machine learning and AGI research. Diving deep into transformer architectures and neuroscience to bridge artificial and human intelligence.
